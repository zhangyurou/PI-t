Recently, diffusion models (DDPMs) have demonstrated their extraordinary generative capabilities, making a significant impact across various fields. However, existing methods often overlook the physical constraints of the robotic arm when generating motions. These methods tend to focus more on result-oriented trajectory generation, leading to outputs that lack physical consistency and adaptability. This paper introduces a physics-informed plug-in model for refining generated trajectories (πt), which incorporates physical information, especially the dynamic characteristics of the robotic arm. The proposed model ensures that the generated trajectory not only effectively completes the task but also adheres to physical constraints during execution, maintaining high smoothness and reliability. To achieve this, we propose a plug-and-play pre-trained module that optimizes trajectory smoothness and temporal action consistency by combining expert demonstration data with the robotic arm's dynamic data. The experimental results show that the proposed method improves task execution accuracy compared to traditional methods and significantly enhances trajectory smoothness. It also demonstrates strong robustness across different environments. This work not only extends the application of physical information in robotic arms but also enhances the rationality and physical interpretability of trajectories, offering new perspectives for future research in robot trajectory planning.

Main Contributions

  • We innovatively use the dynamics prior of the robotic arm as physical information to refine the trajectories generated by the diffusion model, making them more consistent with physical properties.
  • By incorporating a pre-trained model to optimize the generated trajectories, we realize a plug-and-play module, making deployment easier.
  • Based on the existing dataset, we collect the joint information of the robotic arm and compute the corresponding torque, thus increasing the original dataset to support PINN's training.
